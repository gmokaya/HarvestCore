Below is a **detailed design for the TokenHarvest Warehouse Management Module**. This module is the **operational control layer** for warehouses in the platform and supports:

* warehouse dashboards
* commodity intake workflows
* stock reconciliation
* capacity management
* multi-warehouse inventory tracking

It integrates directly with the **Inspection Module, Warehouse Receipt Module, Collateral Engine, Loan Engine, and User/Organization Hierarchy**.

---

# TokenHarvest Warehouse Management Module

## 1. Purpose

The **Warehouse Management Module** manages the full warehouse operations lifecycle for commodities stored under TokenHarvest.

It ensures:

* commodities are properly received and recorded
* stock is traceable across warehouses
* capacity is monitored in real time
* reconciliations detect mismatches early
* inventory supports financing and trading

This module is essential because **warehouse data is the basis for receipts, tokenization, and collateralization**.

---

# 2. Core Functional Areas

The module has five main areas:

1. **Warehouse Dashboards**
2. **Commodity Intake Workflows**
3. **Stock Reconciliation**
4. **Capacity Management**
5. **Multi-Warehouse Inventory Tracking**

---

# 3. Warehouse Dashboards

Provide real-time visibility into warehouse operations.

Used by warehouse managers and staff.

Shows:

* total stock in warehouse

* available capacity
* occupied capacity
* pending intakes
* pending inspections
* receipts issued today
* locked collateral stock
* expiring receipts

Total Capacity: 20,000 Tons
Occupied Capacity: 14,500 Tons
Available Capacity: 5,500 Tons

Maize: 8,000 Tons

Soybeans: 2,000 Tons

### B. Organization Dashboard

Used by warehouse operator organizations managing multiple warehouses.

Shows:

* total stock across all warehouses
* warehouse-by-warehouse utilization

### C. Platform Oversight Dashboard

Used by TokenHarvest operations and compliance teams.

Shows:

* all active warehouses

* warehouse performance metrics

# 4. Commodity Intake Workflows

## Purpose

Ensure every commodity entering a warehouse is consistently recorded, verified, and linked to ownership.

## Intake Workflow

```text
Commodity Arrival
      ↓
Delivery Verification
      ↓
Weighing and Counting
      ↓
Commodity Intake Record
      ↓
Inspection Request
      ↓
Inspection Completion
      ↓
Warehouse Receipt Issuance
      ↓
Inventory Update
```

## Step-by-Step Design

### Step 1: Commodity Arrival

The commodity arrives at the warehouse.

Data captured:

* delivery reference
* supplier / farmer ID
* organization ID
* warehouse ID
* commodity type
* expected quantity
* transport details

---

### Step 2: Delivery Verification

Warehouse intake officer verifies:

* delivery note
* supplier identity
* booking reference
* commodity category

---

### Step 3: Weighing and Counting

System records:

* gross weight
* tare weight
* net weight
* number of bags / bulk quantity
* packaging type

---

### Step 4: Intake Record Creation

An intake record is created.

Example:

```text
Intake ID: INT-2026-0081
Warehouse: WH-KTL-001
Commodity: White Maize
Quantity: 50 Tons
Packaging: 100kg Bags
Owner: FRM-0092
Status: Pending Inspection
```

---

### Step 5: Inspection Trigger

The module automatically creates an inspection request and links it to the intake batch.

---

### Step 6: Receipt Generation

Once inspection passes, the system allows warehouse receipt issuance.

# 6. Stock Reconciliation

## Purpose

Ensure warehouse physical stock matches system stock and receipt-backed stock.

## Reconciliation Types

### A. Daily Operational Reconciliation

Checks:

* today’s intakes
* today’s releases
* current balance

### B. Periodic Physical Reconciliation

Checks:

* physical bag count
* physical weight
* moisture/quality changes
* discrepancies against system inventory

### C. Financial/Collateral Reconciliation

Checks:

* inventory vs warehouse receipts
* inventory vs tokenized receipts
* inventory vs financed stock
* inventory vs locked collateral

---

## Reconciliation Workflow

```text
System Inventory Snapshot
      ↓
Physical Count / Verification
      ↓
Variance Analysis
      ↓
Exception Report
      ↓
Approval / Adjustment
```

## Example Reconciliation Fields

* reconciliation_id
* warehouse_id
* batch_id
* system_quantity
* physical_quantity
* variance
* reconciliation_date
* reconciled_by
* approved_by

Example:

```text
Batch ID: BATCH-0092
System Quantity: 50 Tons
Physical Quantity: 49.5 Tons
Variance: -0.5 Tons
Status: Under Review
```

---

# 7. Reconciliation Controls

To prevent abuse:

* reconciliation adjustments require approval
* high-value variances trigger escalation
* all adjustments logged in audit trail
* financed stock cannot be adjusted without collateral review

Approval roles may include:

* Warehouse Manager
* Operations Supervisor
* Compliance Officer

---

# 8. Capacity Management

## Purpose

Track how much storage is available and prevent over-allocation.

## Capacity Dimensions

The system should track:

* total warehouse capacity
* available capacity
* reserved capacity
* occupied capacity
* blocked/unusable capacity

### Example

```text
Warehouse Capacity: 20,000 Tons
Occupied: 14,500 Tons
Reserved: 2,000 Tons
Available: 3,500 Tons
Blocked: 0
```

---

## Capacity by Zone

Within a warehouse, capacity can also be broken into:

* silo / shed / bay / room / zone

Example:

```text
Warehouse Kitale
├ Bay A – Maize – 5,000 Tons
├ Bay B – Beans – 2,000 Tons
├ Bay C – Soybeans – 1,500 Tons
```

This supports precise storage allocation.

---

## Capacity Alerts

The system should trigger alerts when:

* warehouse reaches 80% utilization
* warehouse reaches 95% utilization
* a commodity zone is full
* space is insufficient for a scheduled intake

---

# 9. Multi-Warehouse Inventory Tracking

## Purpose

Provide centralized visibility across all warehouses linked to an organization or network.

## Views Required

### A. Network Inventory View

Shows all stock across all warehouses.

Example:

```text
Kitale Warehouse – 8,000 Tons Maize
Eldoret Warehouse – 3,500 Tons Beans
Endebess Warehouse – 2,000 Tons Soybeans
```

---

### B. Commodity Distribution View

Shows where each commodity is stored.

Example:

```text
White Maize
- WH-KTL-001: 8,000 Tons
- WH-ELD-002: 2,500 Tons
- WH-END-003: 1,000 Tons
```

---

### C. Ownership View

Shows stock by owner across warehouses.

Example:

```text
Farmer FRM-0092
- Kitale Warehouse: 50 Tons White Maize
- Eldoret Warehouse: 10 Tons Beans
```

---

### D. Financed vs Free Stock View

Shows:

* stock under collateral lock
* stock available for trading
* stock pending inspection
* stock under dispute

---

# 10. Inventory Status Model

Each inventory batch should have a status.

Possible statuses:

```text
Pending Intake
Pending Inspection
Approved
Receipted
Tokenized
Collateral Locked
Reserved for Trade
Released
Under Reconciliation
Disputed
```

This allows every batch to be tracked operationally and financially.
