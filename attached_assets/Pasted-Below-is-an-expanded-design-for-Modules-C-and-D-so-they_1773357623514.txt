Below is an expanded **design for Modules C and D** so they capture **all operational, compliance, traceability, and financing requirements** needed for TokenHarvest. These modules must support **auditable inspection, commodity grading standards, integration with warehouse systems, and generation of a verifiable Digital Warehouse Receipt (DWR)** that can later be **tokenized and used as collateral**.

---

# C. Inspection & Quality Verification Module

## Purpose

The **Inspection & Quality Verification Module** ensures that commodities stored in warehouses meet defined **quality, safety, and grading standards** before a **Digital Warehouse Receipt (DWR)** can be issued.

This module provides:

* **commodity grading and certification**
* **inspection traceability**
* **quality compliance verification**
* **risk assessment for financing**
* **data required for tokenized warehouse receipts**

The module must support **multiple inspection events**, since commodities may be inspected:

* during intake
* during storage
* before delivery
* during collateral verification

---

# 1. Inspection Workflow

```text
Commodity Delivered to Warehouse
        ↓
Initial Inspection
        ↓
Quality Tests Conducted
        ↓
Inspection Report Generated
        ↓
Inspection Approved
        ↓
Digital Warehouse Receipt Issued
```

---

# 2. Inspection Data Captured

### Basic Inspection Data

* **Inspection ID**
* **Commodity Type**
* **Warehouse ID**
* **Warehouse Receipt ID (if available)**
* **Inspection Officer ID**
* **Inspection Date**
* **Inspection Type**

  * Intake inspection
  * Periodic inspection
  * Pre-dispatch inspection
  * Collateral verification inspection

---

# 3. Quality Parameters Captured

For agricultural commodities the system must capture **multiple grading indicators**.

### Moisture & Physical Quality

* Moisture Content (%)
* Broken Grain Percentage
* Foreign Matter (%)
* Pest Damage (%)
* Mold / Aflatoxin Presence
* Discoloration

---

### Commodity Grade

Based on defined standards:

Example for maize:

* Grade A
* Grade B
* Grade C
* Reject

---

### Damage Assessment

* Physical damage
* Insect infestation
* Storage damage
* Handling damage

Damage level classification:

* None
* Minor
* Moderate
* Severe

---

### Weight & Packaging Verification

* Net Weight
* Gross Weight
* Bag Count
* Packaging Type

  * Bags
  * Bulk
  * Pallets

---

### Storage Conditions

* Warehouse Temperature
* Humidity Level
* Storage Method
* Stack Position

These help detect **storage risk that could affect collateral value**.

---

# 4. Certification & Compliance

Inspection module must record certifications.

Examples:

* Export Grade Certification
* Organic Certification
* Phytosanitary Certification
* Warehouse Operator Certification

Fields:

* Certification Type
* Certification Authority
* Certificate Number
* Validity Date

---

# 5. Inspector Verification

Every inspection must be linked to an **authorized inspector**.

Fields include:

* Inspector ID
* Inspector Name
* Organization
* License Number
* Digital Signature

Example:

```text
Inspection Officer: CM-003
Inspector Name: John K. Mwangi
Organization: Kitale Warehouse Inspection Unit
License: INS-8821
```

# 7. Risk Flags for Lending

Inspection module must generate **risk flags**.

Example:

```text
Moisture > 14% → High Storage Risk
Aflatoxin Detected → Financing Not Allowed
Damage Level Severe → Reject
```

These flags feed directly into the **Loan Risk Engine**.

# D. Digital Warehouse Receipt (DWR) Module

## Purpose

The **Digital Warehouse Receipt Module** generates a **digitally verifiable proof of commodity ownership** once inspection is approved.

The receipt serves as:

* proof of ownership
* proof of storage
* proof of commodity quality
* collateral instrument for loans
* asset for marketplace trading
* base asset for tokenization

---

# 1. Receipt Generation Conditions

A DWR can only be issued if:

* commodity intake recorded
* inspection completed
* quality verified
* warehouse authorized

---

# 2. Core Receipt Fields

### Identification Fields

* Receipt ID
* Warehouse Receipt Number
* Registry Reference ID

---

### Commodity Information

* Commodity Type
* Commodity Grade
* Quantity
* Unit of Measure
* Packaging Type

---

### Storage Information

* Warehouse ID
* Warehouse Name
* Storage Location
* Stack Position

---

### Ownership Information

* Owner ID
* Owner Name
* Organization ID (if cooperative)

---

### Inspection Reference

* Inspection Report ID
* Inspector ID
* Inspection Date

---

### Validity Information

* Date Issued
* Expiry Date
* Re-inspection Date

---

# 3. Digital Signature

To prevent fraud, the receipt must contain:

* Warehouse Operator Signature
* Inspection Authority Signature
* Registry Signature (if applicable)

---

# 4. Example Digital Warehouse Receipt

```text

Grade: Grade A
Quantity: 50 Tons

Inspection Report: INS-2026-0031
Inspection Date: 13 July 2026

Date Issued: 14 July 2026
Expiry Date: 14 October 2026
```

---

# 5. Receipt Status States

The DWR must support status tracking.

Possible statuses:

```text
Draft
Active
Collateral Locked
Under Trade
Settled
Expired
Cancelled
```

---

# 6. Blockchain Linkage

Once issued, the receipt can be **tokenized**.

Example token reference:

```text

Blockchain: IOTA
Linked Receipt: WR-2026-0042
Status: Active
```
